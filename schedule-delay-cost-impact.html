Delay Impact, Backed by Records

Construction schedule delay cost impact review connects schedule slippage evidence to cost exposure only when records support the link. Constrovet reviews baseline schedules, revised schedules, progress reports, procurement events, idle resource notes, penalties, and cost records. The output can show days delayed, affected work package, evidence citations, financial impact where available, and missing evidence where cost is not proven. This supports recovery planning without deciding liability or replacing professional project review by project, finance, and legal teams involved.

Problem

Delay information often appears in schedule updates while cost impact appears elsewhere. Procurement delay, idle equipment, labor disruption, penalties, and rework may be discussed separately. Without a connected view, project leaders may know that the project is late but not what delay evidence can support cost review.

How Constrovet helps

  • Compares baseline and revised schedule evidence.
  • Links delay records to procurement, resources, work package, and cost documents.
  • Separates evidenced days from unproven financial impact.
  • Preserves citations and confidence for each delay-related finding.
  • Builds recovery actions around the documented impact.

Data and documents required

  • Baseline schedule, updated schedule, look-ahead plan, and progress reports.
  • Delay notices, meeting minutes, site instructions, and correspondence.
  • Procurement logs, purchase order dates, delivery dates, and vendor notes.
  • Idle resource records, penalty clauses, invoices, and cost summaries.

FAQ

What is schedule delay cost impact?

It is the cost exposure connected to schedule slippage when documents show delay, affected work, resources, procurement, penalties, or other cost evidence.

Can delay days be reported without cost?

Yes. If days are evidenced but cost is not, the output should report days and mark missing cost evidence.

Which documents support delay impact review?

Baseline schedules, revised schedules, progress reports, delay notices, procurement logs, idle resource records, correspondence, and cost records can support review.

Does Constrovet decide delay liability?

No. Constrovet organizes evidence-backed findings for review by project, finance, legal, and executive teams.

How does this connect to recovery planning?

Delay findings can be converted into 7-day evidence checks, 30-day corrective actions, and 90-day monitoring or governance actions.
