Financier Construction Project Risk Audit

Cited cost, schedule, ESG, and recovery intelligence for lender, investor, and board review workflows.

Risk Visibility for Capital Decisions

A financier construction project risk audit helps lenders, investors, and board reviewers understand whether project records show cost leakage, overruns, schedule delay cost impact, ESG evidence gaps, or weak recovery visibility. Constrovet organizes available documents into cited findings with financial category, amount or days where supported, confidence, and missing-evidence notes. It supports due diligence and monitoring, but does not replace formal lender, legal, or technical review by qualified advisors or appointed project consultants responsible for approval.

Problem

Financiers often receive summary dashboards or management updates without enough source evidence. Budget variance, delayed procurement, revised schedules, ESG exposure, and recovery actions may be visible, but not connected. This makes it harder to ask precise questions before funding, restructuring, or project monitoring decisions.

How Constrovet helps

  • Organizes project evidence into cost, leakage, schedule, ESG, and recovery findings.
  • Separates baseline budget from leakage and overrun indicators.
  • Highlights missing evidence that should be requested before decisions.
  • Prepares cited executive findings for financier and advisor review.
  • Supports 7/30/90 recovery action visibility for monitored projects.

Data and documents required

  • Project budgets, BOQs, actual spend records, contracts, and payment records.
  • Progress reports, schedule updates, delay notes, and procurement logs.
  • ESG, fuel, water, energy, waste, and carbon records where available.
  • Existing lender reports, board packs, risk notes, and project correspondence.

FAQ

What is a financier construction project risk audit?

It is an evidence review that helps financiers understand cost, schedule, ESG, recovery, and missing-evidence risks before or during project monitoring.

Does Constrovet replace lender due diligence?

No. Constrovet prepares cited findings for review by financiers and their advisors. It does not replace formal lender, legal, or technical diligence.

What risks can be surfaced?

Potential risks include cost leakage, calculated overruns, schedule delay impact, ESG evidence gaps, procurement delay, and weak recovery visibility.

What documents are required?

Useful documents include budgets, BOQs, progress reports, schedules, procurement records, actual spend records, ESG logs, and board or lender review packs.

Can the output support board reporting?

It can support board review by organizing cited evidence, amounts, days, categories, and actions, subject to stakeholder verification.
