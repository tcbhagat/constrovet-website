Construction Cost Overrun Analysis

Actual vs Budget review for project teams that need calculations tied to source evidence.

The overrun formula

When both Budget and Actual are available in the supplied evidence, Constrovet calculates overrun as Actual - Budget. If Actual is greater than Budget, the finding is treated separately from baseline budget and tagged as leakage and overrun.

This keeps the calculation transparent for finance and project review. It also prevents contract value, planned spend, BOQ value, cumulative work done, or advance recovery from being mislabeled as leakage.

What evidence is needed

  • A budget, estimate, BOQ, approved baseline, or planned spend figure.
  • An actual, certified, paid, consumed, billed, or incurred amount.
  • A source reference showing file, page or sheet, and quoted span.
  • Context that identifies the work package, period, item, or project component.

Why citations matter

Overrun findings can affect board conversations, lender reviews, contractor meetings, and recovery decisions. Citations make the number reviewable and reduce reliance on unsupported summary statements.

Output discipline

Do not calculate overrun when one side of the formula is missing. The output should mark the missing evidence.

Discuss overrun review

Is overrun always a cash leakage?

No. The calculation identifies a variance. The category depends on the available evidence and should not be expanded beyond it.

Can planned spend be tagged as leakage?

No. Planned spend, contract value, BOQ value, cumulative work done, and advance recovery are baseline budget signals.
