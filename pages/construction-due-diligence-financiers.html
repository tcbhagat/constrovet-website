In one paragraph

In one paragraph: Constrovet supports financier construction project risk review by organizing project documents into cited baseline budget, overrun, leakage, ESG, schedule, and recovery findings. It prepares an evidence brief for lenders, investors, boards, and advisors, but does not replace formal lender diligence, legal review, valuation, or technical due diligence by qualified professionals.

What financiers need from project documents

Financiers usually need a clear view of whether project records show baseline budget, actual spend, cost leakage, overrun, ESG metrics, schedule slippage, and recovery priorities. Constrovet structures those answers with citations.

Due diligence questions Constrovet supports

  • Which findings are baseline budget and which are leakage or overrun?
  • Do Budget and Actual values support an Actual - Budget overrun calculation?
  • Are ESG metrics evidenced, missing, or mixed into unrelated financial claims?
  • Which 7/30/90-day actions should leadership review first?

Where it fits in the decision cycle

Use the workflow before new funding, during project monitoring, when delays trigger governance review, or when stakeholders need a neutral evidence brief before negotiations.

Evidence standard

Every finding should include file, page or sheet, quoted span, amount or days where available, confidence, and missing-evidence notes.

Is Constrovet a replacement for lender diligence?

No. Constrovet is a project intelligence workflow that prepares cited findings for review by the financier and its advisors.

Can the output support board reporting?

It can support board-ready review by organizing evidence, amounts, days, categories, and actions, subject to stakeholder verification.
