How Constrovet Works

From raw site records to cited executive recovery actions in four practical steps.

In one paragraph

In one paragraph: Constrovet works by ingesting available construction project documents, classifying baseline budget, leakage, overrun, schedule, and ESG evidence, generating cited findings with calculations where records support them, and turning those findings into 7/30/90-day action plans for executive review. It is designed to show what the documents support, what is missing, and what should be reviewed next.

Document-to-Action Workflow

Each step keeps source evidence visible for project, finance, and executive review.

1

Ingest Project Evidence

Upload or connect available project files: budgets, BOQs, procurement logs, progress reports, schedules, material records, ESG logs, and related documents.

2

Classify Budget, Leakage & ESG

The workflow separates baseline budget values from leakage and overrun indicators, while ESG metrics remain in their own evidence category.

3

Generate Cited Findings

Findings include source file, page or sheet, quoted span, confidence, and calculations such as Actual - Budget where both values are available.

4

Produce 7/30/90-Day Action Plans

Leadership receives phased actions tied to the cited findings, helping teams decide what to review, recover, negotiate, or monitor next.

FAQ

What documents can Constrovet review?

Typical inputs include budgets, BOQs, actual spend records, procurement files, schedules, progress reports, ESG logs, and site notes.

Does Constrovet guess missing values?

No. Missing evidence should be stated clearly instead of creating unsupported amounts or causes.

What is the final output?

The intended output is a cited executive report with findings, calculations where supported, missing-evidence notes, and 7/30/90 actions.
