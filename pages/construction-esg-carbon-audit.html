Construction ESG & Carbon Audit

Extract environmental and compliance-related metrics from project records without mixing them with financial leakage.

What ESG metrics can be captured?

When documents contain the values, Constrovet can structure carbon, waste diversion, energy, water, fuel, diesel, electricity, and emissions metrics as ESG findings. These remain separate from baseline budget and leakage categories.

Useful source records

  • Diesel, fuel, electricity, and equipment consumption records.
  • Water usage, waste handling, and waste diversion logs.
  • Material quantities and carbon-related declarations where available.
  • Project compliance notes, inspection records, and site reports.

Why separate ESG from leakage?

ESG metrics may influence project governance, lender reporting, and regulatory review, but they should not be treated as cost leakage unless the evidence also supports a financial leakage finding.

Honesty check

If carbon, fuel, water, energy, or waste evidence is missing, the report should state that gap rather than invent a metric.

Does Constrovet claim automatic compliance certification?

No. The public website describes evidence extraction and review workflows, not a blanket certification or assurance.

Can ESG data support executive actions?

Yes, when cited ESG findings indicate a review, documentation, monitoring, or recovery action for the project team.
