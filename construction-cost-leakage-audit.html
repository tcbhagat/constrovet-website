Cost Leakage, Clearly Separated

A construction cost leakage audit reviews project documents to identify where money may be lost beyond the approved baseline. Constrovet separates standard contract value, BOQ value, planned spend, cumulative work done, and advance recovery from actual leakage indicators. It then organizes evidence-backed findings such as penalties, wastage, rework, delayed purchase order impact, idle resources, excess consumption, and calculated overruns into a reviewable executive format with citations and missing-evidence notes for India-first project governance and accountable recovery decisions by leadership.

Problem

Cost leakage is often hidden because project teams see totals, not causes. Budget variance, procurement delay, material consumption, rework, and schedule slippage may sit in separate files. Executives may receive a high-level cost report without knowing which items are baseline budget and which items need recovery review.

How Constrovet helps

  • Classifies baseline budget separately from leakage and overrun signals.
  • Calculates overrun as Actual - Budget when both values are available.
  • Connects leakage findings to source file, page or sheet, and quoted span.
  • Marks missing evidence instead of inventing unsupported values.
  • Converts priority findings into 7/30/90 action relevance for executive review.

Data and documents required

  • Approved budget, BOQ, contract value, planned spend, and actual spend records.
  • Purchase orders, invoices, material consumption logs, and stock records.
  • Progress reports, delay notes, site instructions, and correspondence.
  • Penalty, rework, wastage, idle resource, and excess consumption evidence.

FAQ

What is construction cost leakage?

Construction cost leakage is evidence-backed value loss such as penalties, wastage, rework, idle resources, delayed procurement impact, excess consumption, or calculated overrun.

Is every overrun leakage?

No. Overrun is calculated when Budget and Actual values are both available. Whether it is leakage depends on the evidence and review context.

What documents are useful for a leakage audit?

Useful documents include BOQs, budgets, actual spend records, purchase orders, invoices, material logs, progress reports, delay notes, and site correspondence.

Can Constrovet estimate missing amounts?

Constrovet should not invent missing amounts. If evidence is missing, the finding should show the gap.

Who reviews the findings?

Findings are prepared for review by project owners, contractors, project managers, consultants, financiers, and executive teams.
